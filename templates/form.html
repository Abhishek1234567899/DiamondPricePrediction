Diamond Price Prediction Form

Feature Descriptions

Carat: The weight of the diamond, where one carat equals 1/5 gram. Typically ranges from 0.2 to 5.0 carats for an outstanding diamond (float).

Depth: The total depth percentage of the diamond, calculated as (z / mean(x, y)). Typically ranges from 55% to 65% for a well-shaped diamond.

Table: The width of the diamond's table expressed as a percentage of its average diameter. Typically ranges from 50% to 70%, particularly for a well-shaped diamond.

x: The length of the diamond in mm, typically ranging from 3.5mm to 10.5mm for a well-shaped diamond (float).

y: The width of the diamond in mm. Typically ranges from 3.5mm to 10.5mm for a well-shaped diamond (float).

z: The depth of the diamond in mm. Typically ranges from 2.0mm to 6.5mm for a well-shaped diamond (float).

Cut: The quality of the diamond's cut (categorical):

  • Fair: Below average cut quality.
  • Good: Average cut quality.
  • Very Good: Above average cut quality.
  • Premium: High-quality cut, excellent brilliance.
  • Ideal: The best quality cut, maximum brilliance.

Color: The color grade of the diamond:

  • D: Absolutely colorless. The highest color grade, extremely rare.
  • E: Colorless. Only minute traces of color can be detected by an expert gemologist. A rare diamond.
  • F: Colorless. Slight color detected by an expert gemologist, but still considered a "colorless" grade. High-quality diamond.
  • G: Near-colorless. Color is noticeable only when compared to diamonds of better grades. Excellent value.
  • H: Near-colorless. Color is slightly detectable, but offers good value.
  • I: Near-colorless. Slightly more detectable color, but a great value.
  • J: Near-colorless. Noticeable color, but still offers good value.

Clarity: The clarity grade of the diamond:

  • I1: Inclusions are visible to the naked eye.
  • SI2: Slightly Included 2. Inclusions are noticeable under 10x magnification.
  • SI1: Slightly Included 1. Inclusions are noticeable under 10x magnification.
  • VS2: Very Slightly Included 2. Inclusions are minor and range from difficult to somewhat easy to see for a skilled grader using 10x magnification.
  • VS1: Very Slightly Included 1. Inclusions are difficult to see under 10x magnification.
  • VVS2: Very Very Slightly Included 2. Inclusions are very difficult to see even under 10x magnification.
  • VVS1: Very Very Slightly Included 1. Inclusions are extremely difficult to see under 10x magnification.
  • IF: Internally Flawless. No inclusions visible under 10x magnification.
